Fentanyl Quantitation Device Project

Fall 2021-Winter 2022

In each of the 4th and 5th year of the engineering physics program at UBC, teams of 4 take on an industry-sponsored project designed to give students an opportunity to tackle an ambitious real-world problem.

My 3 teammates and I are currrently working on a novel fentanyl quantitation device sponsored by two UBC chemistry professors. The device is designed to fill an urgent need in the opioid epidemic to provide quick and accurate fentanyl measurements of a drug sample to reduce overdose deaths in BC and beyond.

We are already well underway on the project, and we've spent a lot of time interfacing the various components of the device. By the end of March we're aiming to have a working protoype. We're excited to produce a tool that will make a real difference in our community and save many lives.

Stay tuned for more updates!
